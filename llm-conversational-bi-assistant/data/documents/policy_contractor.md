# Contractor Policies & Guidelines

**Document ID:** POL-CTR-001  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Last Reviewed:** January 10, 2026  
**Owner:** Legal & Compliance

## Employment Classification

All contractors placed through our staffing services are W-2 employees of our organization, not the client. As such, they are entitled to the benefits and protections outlined in this document. Contractors are NOT independent contractors (1099) unless explicitly stated in their placement agreement.

## Time-Off Policy

Contractors accrue paid time off (PTO) at a rate of 1 hour per 30 hours worked, up to a maximum of 40 hours per year. PTO accrual begins on the contractor's start date.

**Requesting Time Off:**
- Contractors must submit time-off requests at least 5 business days in advance through the VMS portal or by contacting their staffing coordinator.
- Approval is subject to client operational needs and must be confirmed by the on-site supervisor.
- Unused PTO is paid out upon assignment completion or termination.

**Holidays:** Contractors are not automatically entitled to paid holidays unless specified in the client contract. The following holidays are observed as unpaid days off for all contractors: New Year's Day, Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving Day, and Christmas Day.

## Attendance & Punctuality

Contractors are expected to adhere to the work schedule defined in their placement agreement. Unexcused absences or chronic tardiness may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of the assignment.

**No-Call, No-Show Policy:** If a contractor fails to report to work without prior notification for two consecutive days, the placement may be terminated immediately. The staffing coordinator must attempt to contact the contractor within 4 hours of the first no-show.

## Workplace Conduct

Contractors must comply with all client workplace policies, including dress code, safety protocols, and code of conduct. Violations are subject to our progressive discipline policy:

1. Verbal warning (documented)
2. Written warning
3. Final written warning / performance improvement plan
4. Termination of assignment

Serious violations (harassment, theft, safety violations, substance abuse) may result in immediate termination without progressive discipline.

## Benefits

Contractors working 30+ hours per week are eligible for:

- **Health Insurance:** Medical, dental, and vision coverage through our group plan. Enrollment is available after 60 days of continuous employment. Open enrollment occurs annually in November.
- **401(k) Retirement Plan:** Eligible after 6 months of continuous employment with a minimum of 1,000 hours worked. The company matches 50% of contributions up to 3% of gross pay.
- **Workers' Compensation:** All contractors are covered from day one.
- **Short-Term Disability:** Available after 90 days of continuous employment.

## Confidentiality

Contractors may have access to confidential client information during their assignments. All contractors sign a confidentiality and non-disclosure agreement as part of onboarding. Breach of confidentiality is grounds for immediate termination and may result in legal action.

## Assignment Completion

When a contractor's assignment ends:

1. Return all client property (badges, equipment, access cards) on the last day.
2. Complete a final timesheet for hours worked in the last pay period.
3. Participate in an exit interview with the staffing coordinator (optional but encouraged).
4. COBRA information will be sent within 14 days if the contractor was enrolled in health benefits.

The staffing coordinator must update the contractor's status in Job Diva within 24 hours of assignment completion.
