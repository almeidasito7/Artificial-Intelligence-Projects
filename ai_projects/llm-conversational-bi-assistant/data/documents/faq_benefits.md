# Frequently Asked Questions: Contractor Benefits

**Document ID:** FAQ-BEN-001  
**Last Updated:** February 20, 2026  
**Owner:** Human Resources

## Health Insurance

**Q: When am I eligible for health insurance?**  
A: You become eligible after 60 days of continuous employment working 30 or more hours per week. Enrollment materials are sent to your email on your eligibility date.

**Q: What plans are available?**  
A: We offer three medical plan options through Aetna: a PPO plan, an HMO plan, and a High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) with HSA. Dental coverage is through Delta Dental, and vision coverage is through VSP.

**Q: How much does health insurance cost?**  
A: Employee-only premiums start at $45/week for the HMO plan, $62/week for the PPO plan, and $28/week for the HDHP. Family coverage is available at higher rates. Detailed rate sheets are provided during enrollment.

**Q: What happens to my health insurance when my assignment ends?**  
A: You will receive COBRA continuation information within 14 days of your last day. COBRA allows you to continue your coverage for up to 18 months at full premium cost plus a 2% administrative fee. If you are placed on a new assignment within 30 days, your coverage continues without interruption.

## Retirement (401k)

**Q: When can I enroll in the 401(k)?**  
A: You are eligible after 6 months of continuous employment and 1,000 hours worked. Enrollment is available the first of the month following your eligibility date.

**Q: Is there a company match?**  
A: Yes. The company matches 50% of your contributions up to 3% of your gross pay. For example, if you contribute 3% of your pay, the company adds an additional 1.5%. The match vests over 3 years: 33% after year 1, 66% after year 2, and 100% after year 3.

**Q: Can I roll over a 401(k) from a previous employer?**  
A: Yes. Contact our 401(k) administrator (Fidelity) to initiate a rollover. There are no fees for incoming rollovers.

## Paid Time Off

**Q: How does PTO accrual work?**  
A: You accrue 1 hour of PTO for every 30 hours worked, up to 40 hours per year. Accrual begins on your first day of work.

**Q: How do I request time off?**  
A: Submit your request at least 5 business days in advance through the VMS portal or by contacting your staffing coordinator. Your on-site supervisor must approve the request.

**Q: Are holidays paid?**  
A: Paid holidays depend on your client contract. Some clients provide paid holidays for contractors; others do not. Check with your staffing coordinator for your specific assignment. All contractors have 6 standard holidays as unpaid days off (New Year's, Memorial Day, July 4th, Labor Day, Thanksgiving, Christmas).

**Q: What happens to unused PTO?**  
A: Unused PTO is paid out when your assignment ends. PTO does not carry over between assignments unless you are re-placed within 30 days.

## Workers' Compensation

**Q: Am I covered if I get injured at work?**  
A: Yes. All contractors are covered by workers' compensation insurance from day one. If you are injured on the job, report the injury to your on-site supervisor and your staffing coordinator immediately. Do not delay reporting — late reports can complicate your claim.

**Q: Does workers' comp cover commuting injuries?**  
A: Generally, no. Workers' compensation covers injuries that occur during the course and scope of your work duties. Injuries during your normal commute are not typically covered.

## Payroll

**Q: When do I get paid?**  
A: Contractors are paid weekly via direct deposit every Friday for timesheets submitted and approved by Monday at 12:00 PM EST of that week.

**Q: What if my timesheet is late?**  
A: Late timesheets are processed in the following pay cycle. If you consistently submit timesheets late, your staffing coordinator will work with you to resolve the issue.

**Q: How do I set up direct deposit?**  
A: Direct deposit is set up during onboarding. You can update your banking information at any time by contacting payroll at payroll@company.com. Changes take effect in the next pay cycle.

**Q: How do I access my pay stubs and tax documents?**  
A: Pay stubs are available through our employee self-service portal (ADP Workforce Now). W-2 forms are mailed by January 31 and are also available electronically through the portal.
